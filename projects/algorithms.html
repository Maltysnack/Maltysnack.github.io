Sliding-tile puzzle

Start with a row of tiles in two colours plus one blank, arranged at random. On every turn, one tile moves into the blank space. Three moves are legal:

  • Slidecost 1an adjacent tile shifts into the blank
  • Hop onecost 2a tile jumps over one tile to land in the blank
  • Hop twocost 3a tile jumps over two tiles to land in the blank

Put every tile of the first colour to the left of every tile of the second. The blank can finish anywhere. The cost-aware algorithms (A*, Greedy, Beam) minimise total cost; iterative deepening treats every move as one and minimises step count.

Tour planning

Visit every point and return to where you started, with the shortest total tour. Even at 12 points, that's 240 million possible orderings, far too many to check exhaustively. Both algorithms below sample many candidate tours and keep improving the best one they find.

Click in the canvas to add a point, click an existing one to remove it. Hit Run: the algorithm searches for ~100 iterations, then draws its best tour starting from the marked node, one edge at a time.
