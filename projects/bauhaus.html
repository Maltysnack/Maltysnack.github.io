Exhibition

Bauhaus

Bauhaus ran from 1919 to 1933 across three cities and three directors, and it was many incompatible things across those years. This exhibition walks through that building room by room, in rough chronological order.

1919 - 1933+14 years11 rooms

The mid-century synthesis that turned the school into a single visual style, primary colours on a grid, is one room in a much larger building. The painting workshops, the weaving workshop, the stage workshop, the photographers, the Marxist functionalists, and the architects all worked under the same roof and disagreed about almost everything. The exhibition takes that contradiction seriously.

Some rooms have working generators; the ones whose visual logic is rule-based enough to model in code. Others are essays only; the ones whose work depended on hand, intuition, photography, or politics rather than measurable rules. Both kinds of room are honest, and the rhythm of switching between them is the point.

01

Foundations: Itten's Vorkurs

1919 - 1922 / Weimar

Expressionist exercises, mystical Vorkurs, hand-felt forms. Johannes Itten led the preliminary course in robes; students fasted on a Mazdaznan diet and did breathing exercises before drawing. Almost nothing of the canonical Bauhaus look exists yet.

The Turn

1923 / Weimar

The pivot. Theo van Doesburg's De Stijl ideas pour in. Moholy-Nagy replaces Itten in the Vorkurs. The first Bauhaus Exhibition unveils the geometric-primary direction that will become the school's signature.

→ 03

Dessau Vorkurs

1925 - 1928 / Dessau

The era we picture when we picture Bauhaus. Geometric primaries on a paper ground, asymmetric balance, the typography workshop's grid discipline. A five-level generator builds from random shapes to balanced composition, one rule at a time.

Klee's Room

painting workshop / 1921 - 1931

Muted palettes, magic-square grids, hand-felt geometry. The generator approximates Klee's late-Bauhaus structural mode (Polyphony, Ad Parnassum). An SVG turbulence filter fakes the bleed; the essay carries what the filter cannot.

→ 05

Kandinsky's Room

painting workshop / 1922 - 1933

Outlined shapes, overlapping ellipses, the play of weights and tensions. Point and Line to Plane is essentially a treatise on generating composition by rule. Near-black ground, wider palette, diagonals cutting through.

generator live
→ 06

The Weaving Workshop

Stolzl, Albers, Berger / 1919 - 1933

The largest workshop by enrolment, almost entirely women, long dismissed as "women's craft" and recently reappraised as one of the most rigorous abstract design programmes the school ran. Loom logic, banded structures, earth palettes. A non-uniform grid of cells with one dominant colour per column.

→ 07

Schlemmer's Stage

stage workshop / 1923 - 1929

Triadic Ballet, figural silhouettes, dancers as geometric solids. The generator places one to three mannequins on a stage: sphere head, trapezoid torso, bar limbs, costume-coloured. The body as architecture.

hybrid live
→ 08

Photograms

Moholy-Nagy, L. Moholy, Peterhans / 1922 - 1933

Lensless photography. Semi-transparent silhouettes on a dark ground; overlap brightens via screen blending, simulating multiple light passes through translucent objects. No colour, no primary palette.

→ 09

Hannes Meyer's Turn

directorship / 1928 - 1930

The Marxist directorship the popular histories skip. Less aesthetics, more sociology: working-class housing, mass-produced furniture, the ADGB Trade Union School at Bernau. No generator; this is documentary, not style.

→ 10

Mies and the Architectural Exit

directorship / 1930 - 1933

Plans, elevations, axonometrics. The generator produces an orthogonal elevation: building rectangle, regular grid of glass bays, ground line, occasional dimension marks. Less is more, three years from closure.

generator live
→ 11

The Diaspora

1933+ / Chicago, Aspen, Black Mountain, Cambridge MA

The school is closed under the Nazis in 1933 and its faculty scatter. Gropius to Harvard, Moholy-Nagy to the New Bauhaus, Albers to Black Mountain then Yale, Mies to IIT, Bayer to Aspen. The "Bauhaus look" becomes the International Style.

Eleven rooms, finished as of 11-05-2026. Five with full generators, two hybrids, four essays. The synthesis we inherited was always partial; the exhibition is the long version. Every room can change as new sources or new ideas arrive.
