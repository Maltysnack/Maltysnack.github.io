Duoclue · board mockups

Three directions for the board redesign. Sample length 32 squares, 3 teams.

Option A

Serpentine snake

PROS Easy to read at phone size · every square always faces up · clear progression direction · feels like a Snakes & Ladders / Mario Party board · scales cleanly to 24/32/40 by adding rows.
CONS Loses the "spiral" identity that Duoclue had as a motif · feels more "kids' game" and less "puzzle."
Option B

Flat spiral · drawn path

PROS Keeps the spiral motif · path-as-ribbon trick reads as "board" much better than disconnected dots · zero perspective tricks so icons stay legible · cheapest change.
CONS Inner squares still small · spiral is genuinely hard to fit at landscape phone scale · feels less "fun" than (A) or (C).
Option C

Winding road · Mario Party island

PROS Most "board game" feel · the island context (tree clumps, road dashes, water edge) sells the world without expensive 3D · path-as-ribbon makes movement direction obvious · most distinctive visually.
CONS Most work to implement well · placing 32–40 squares organically along an island path needs hand-tuning · risk of looking cluttered at small sizes if not careful · doesn't scale to longer boards as cleanly as (A).

Pick A, B, or C — or mix (e.g. "C's island vibe, but with A's serpentine layout"). Then I implement and ship.
