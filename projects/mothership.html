Project — Game

Mothership

Built in LÖVE2D (Lua) · Strategic card game

Status In development
Engine LÖVE2D
Platform Desktop
Direction Roguelike deck builder

Mothership is a two-player strategic card game with a roguelike structure. Each player builds an army of cards around their mothership — defence on the left, attack on the right. To win, you destroy the opponent's mothership. But to reach it, you must dismantle everything in front of it first.

How it plays

  • Each player places their mothership card at the centre of their field
  • Cards are connected by overlapping into corner slots: same number connects in the upper corner, same suit or colour in the lower corner
  • Only cards with nothing on top of them can attack or be attacked — you build in layers
  • To attack: select your attacker and a target, then both players flip the top card of their deck — highest combined total wins that fight
  • You must destroy all defence cards before you can attack the mothership
  • Factions: Human Unity, Human Terra, Alien Prime, Alien Nova — each with distinct card art colours
  • The Jack card is wild — can connect to anything
Devlog #2

Combat is working, factions are in

12 Mar 2026

The core battle system is now functional. Cards snap into corner slots correctly, the layer system works (only exposed cards are interactive), and the combat resolution — flip top of deck, compare totals, winner stands — plays out with a brief animated state.

Four factions are implemented: Human Unity (blue/navy), Human Terra (blue/teal), Alien Prime (grey/purple) and Alien Nova (grey/red). The Jack card is wild and connects into any slot regardless of number or suit. A full standard 52-card deck is in use with shuffle and deal working cleanly.

Next: a proper turn structure (currently free-placement), a basic opponent AI, and the roguelike run layer — encounter chains, card rewards, and meta progression. The bones are solid. Time to put walls around them.
