Most card games ask you to build the perfect deck. Mothership asks you to defend what you have and destroy what they have, lane by lane, card by card. Four factions. One board. No deck-building required.

Mothership is a two-player strategic card game with a roguelike structure. Each player builds an army of cards around their mothership — defence on the left, attack on the right. To win, you destroy the opponent's mothership. But to reach it, you must dismantle everything in front of it first.

The Board

A 5×5 grid. Four corner slots score points. The centre is the Mothership.

🃏 THE WILD JACK

The Jack card belongs to no faction and serves all of them.

Play it in any lane. It adapts to the strongest adjacent card's faction type.

How to Play

1
🃏 Deal the cards
Each player draws 7 cards from their faction deck and places their Mothership at the centre slot.
2
⚔️ Place and attack
On your turn, place a card in any empty lane. Cards connect via corner slots — same number upper corner, same suit lower corner.
3
🏰 Control the corners
Only cards with nothing on top can attack or be attacked. Build in layers to protect your key pieces.
4
🎯 Destroy the Mothership
Both players flip the top card of their deck — highest combined total wins the fight. Destroy all defence cards, then strike the Mothership to win.

Factions

Four factions. Each with a distinct worldview and card art palette.

Human Unity
"Control the board. Control the future."
The Unity council believes civilisation survives only through coordination — every card placed in service of a larger grid. They do not fight alone; they fight as a formation, and their strength compounds with every connected position.
Human Terra
"The earth remembers. So do we."
Terra's soldiers fight for reclaimed ground, each lane representing soil that was lost and must be won back. Their cards are slower to place but deeply rooted — dislodging a Terra position costs more than it should.
Alien Prime
"Logic is the only weapon worth carrying."
Prime calculates every placement like a proof — no card is placed without a determined outcome. They regard the corner slots as variables to be solved, and the Mothership as an equation that ends the game.
Alien Nova
"Change is not a threat. It is a strategy."
Nova shifts and mutates mid-game, cards that were defensive becoming offensive as conditions evolve. Their decks feel unstable to opponents precisely because they are — and that instability is the plan.

Devlog

Devlog #2 12 Mar 2026 Combat System

Combat is working, factions are in

The core battle system is now functional. Cards snap into corner slots correctly, the layer system works (only exposed cards are interactive), and the combat resolution — flip top of deck, compare totals, winner stands — plays out with a brief animated state.

Four factions are implemented: Human Unity (blue/navy), Human Terra (blue/teal), Alien Prime (grey/purple) and Alien Nova (grey/red). The Jack card is wild and connects into any slot regardless of number or suit. A full standard 52-card deck is in use with shuffle and deal working cleanly.

Next: a proper turn structure (currently free-placement), a basic opponent AI, and the roguelike run layer — encounter chains, card rewards, and meta progression. The bones are solid. Time to put walls around them.

Devlog #1 10 Feb 2025 Foundation

Core loop is alive

Got the core battle loop working and the roguelike structure drafted. Cards can be placed, corner slots snap correctly, and the mothership placement is fixed at centre field. The card renderer is handling dynamic colour fills via LÖVE2D's built-in draw calls — less elegant than I wanted but it works.

Next milestone: the full first encounter chain, then a basic meta-progression layer.

Next milestone

Playable single-round browser prototype using the core combat system.
