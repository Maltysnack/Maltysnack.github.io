# SideQuest — Class Trees

## Structure

7 class paths total. 3 base classes (one habit each). 3 duo classes (two habits each). 1 prestige path (all three habits). Each path has 4 stages. You advance by maintaining your streak — longer streaks push you to higher stages.

Citizen is the starting state, not a class path. It has no stages.

---

## Unlock mechanics

**Base class unlock:** 3 consecutive days of the class daily → enter Stage 1
**Duo class unlock:** 5 consecutive days of BOTH class dailies simultaneously → enter Stage 1
**Prestige unlock:** Reach Stage 3 in ALL three base classes → enter prestige Stage 1

**Stage advancement (base classes):**
| Stage | Days maintained |
|---|---|
| Stage 1 | 3 days (unlock) |
| Stage 2 | 14 days |
| Stage 3 | 30 days |
| Stage 4 | 90 days |

**Stage advancement (duo classes):**
| Stage | Days maintained (both habits) |
|---|---|
| Stage 1 | 5 days |
| Stage 2 | 21 days |
| Stage 3 | 45 days |
| Stage 4 | 120 days |

## Prestige path — The Eternal ⭐

**Unlock condition:** Reach Stage 3 (Scholar / Knight / Ranger, 30 days each) in ALL three base classes simultaneously.
This means you're reading every day, exercising every day, AND going outside every day, all for 30 days. This is rare. This should feel like a genuine achievement.

**All five dailies XP bonus:** +20% on top of all other bonuses.

| Stage | Title | Condition | Flavour |

| 3 | **Mythic** | All three at 90 days | "They write songs about people like you. Quietly." |

## Sleep and water — universal buffs (not class paths)

These don't have class paths. They're environmental bonuses that affect everyone equally.

| Daily | Buff | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Rest at the Inn (sleep 8h) | +20% to all XP earned today | 24 hours from completion |
| Drink from the Sacred Spring (2L water) | +10% to all XP earned today | 24 hours from completion |

**Stack:** Both active = +30% to everything, on top of all class bonuses. This rewards the foundational habits first, before even thinking about classes.

---

## Full XP stack example

**User: Templar Stage 3 (45-day streak, exercise + reading both maintained), both sleep and water done today**

- Study Ancient Tomes: 25 XP base
- × 1.20 (slept) = 30
- × 1.10 (hydrated) = 33
- × 1.50 (Acolyte class bonus) = 49.5
- × 1.50 (Knight bonus on training — same person, different daily)
- × 1.15 (45-day streak milestone bonus)
- **Result: ~57 XP on the study quest, ~65 XP on the training quest**

The gap between a fully-buffed Templar and a new Citizen doing minimum effort is about 2.5×. Enough to feel meaningful. Not so much that it punishes new users.

## Feats

Feats are secondary traits — things you do sometimes, not every day. They sit alongside your class without replacing it.

**The distinction:**
- **Classes** = daily habits, earned through streaks. Your core identity.
- **Feats** = occasional habits, earned through cumulative completions. Your texture.

A Knight who draws sometimes. A Druid who cooks. An Acolyte who plays guitar. Feats let that complexity show without requiring a daily commitment.

### Feat mechanics

- **Unlock:** Complete the associated activity 5 times (non-consecutive, no streak required)
- **Maintain:** Complete it at least once per week (soft — not punishing to miss)
- **Effect:** Profile badge, flavour text tag, small perk (not class-level XP bonuses — feats are additive, not competitive)
- **Stack:** You can hold as many feats as you've earned

### Feat list

| Feat | Activity | Unlock | Perk |
|---|---|---|---|
| 🎨 Artist | Create something (draw, paint, make) | 5 completions | "The Inspired" profile tag + creative flavour on quest log |
| 🍳 Chef | Cook from scratch | 5 completions | +5% XP on water/nourishment dailies |
| 📓 Scribe | Write in a journal or chronicle | 5 completions | +5% XP on study quests |
| 🎵 Bard | Play or practice an instrument / perform | 5 completions | Musical flavour text on quest completion messages |
| 🧘 Monk | Meditate | 5 completions | Silent built-in streak grace day (not advertised) |
| 🤝 Herald | Meaningful social interaction | 5 completions | Rare "fellowship bonus" event (+XP burst) |
| 🧹 Steward | Clean or organise your space | 5 completions | Small gold find bonus on return journeys |

### Feat → Class graduation (future)

If someone does a feat activity consistently enough, it can graduate into a full class path. Threshold: 30 feat completions → feat promoted to class. Same titles, higher stakes.

This keeps feats honest — they're not permanent labels, they're earned and maintained. But they don't punish you with streaks you're not ready to commit to.

## Naming notes / future expansion

The current three base paths map directly to the three core habits. Future habits would introduce future classes (or begin as feats that graduate):

| Potential future daily | Class path | Title progression | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Prepare a Meal (cook) | Alchemist | Apprentice → Herbalist → Alchemist → Grand Alchemist | Chef feat first |
| Write in the Chronicle (journal) | Scribe | Novice → Scribe → Chronicler → Loremaster | Scribe feat first |
| Meditate at Dawn | Shaman | Caller → Shaman → Elder → Spiritwalker | Monk feat first; avoid Druid conflict |
| Speak with a Stranger (social) | Bard | Minstrel → Bard → Herald → Storian | Herald feat first |
| Create something (art/music/craft) | Artist | Apprentice → Artisan → Artist → Virtuoso | Artist feat first |

These create more duo and trio combinations downstream. The system can expand indefinitely as new habits are added. Feats are the on-ramp to new classes — you don't get a class on day one, you get a feat, and if the habit sticks, it earns its title.
