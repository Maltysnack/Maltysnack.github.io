# YONDER — GAME DESCRIPTION

## One-line pitch
A quiet, atmospheric idle RPG about a long road, a small character, and an ending that was always coming.

## Short description (handoff / vision doc)
Yonder is a single-page browser idle RPG with no framework dependencies — one HTML file, vanilla JS and CSS. The player creates a character in a frontier town called Dawnhearth and sets out on the King's Road.

The game is structured around missions picked up from a noticeboard — jobs, bounties, quests, and eventually adventures that carry the player forward to the next town. Combat is a turn-based simulation that plays out automatically and replays as a log. Between missions, the player buys gear, trains class abilities, drinks at the tavern, prays at the church, and watches the world accumulate in a diary and a chronicle.

There are four towns on the road: Dawnhearth, Millriver, Grimhollow, and Farradin. Each town has its own palette, its own buildings, its own character classes to unlock, and its own tone — frontier warmth, river fog, forest rot, cold capital stone. The player moves forward one-way. They don't go back.

The writing is entirely in world voice. Nothing breaks the fourth wall. The player reads a kin line from an NPC, a cryptic quote from the moon, a terse diary entry written in character. The game never says "you have unlocked the Knight class" — it shows you a figure in the Ember Sanctum who has been waiting.

The ending is a cursed king in a castle at the end of the road, who asks you not to stop.

## Steam-style description
Yonder is a slow, text-driven idle RPG. You will spend most of your time watching a progress bar move.

Pick up work from the noticeboard. Run jobs, hunt bounties, take on quests. Earn gold. Buy better gear. Train your abilities. Unlock new classes — each one a person you become through choices made in taverns, shrines, and dark forest roads.

The world is written, not explained. You'll learn what things mean by finding them. You'll learn what the moon says if you ask her. You'll learn what Farradin is when you arrive, and what the king is when you face him.

Four towns. One road. A character who dies and leaves a headstone, and a bloodline that carries something forward.

**Features**
- Single-file browser game. No install. No account. Just open and play.
- Four towns with distinct visual palettes, buildings, and tones.
- Nine playable classes, each unlocked through the world rather than a menu.
- Turn-based combat simulation with full replay log.
- A persistent chronicle, a graveyard, and heirlooms that outlive their owners.
- An ending.
